What's Happening in New Port Richey This March (2026)

Spring is here, and New Port Richey is packed with things to do. Whether you're a local business owner looking for networking opportunities or just trying to get out of the house, here's what's worth your time in March 2026.

Gasparilla Festival of the Arts — Feb 28 – Mar 1

Kicking things off right at the end of February, the Gasparilla Festival of the Arts returns to Julian B. Lane Waterfront Park in Tampa. This is one of the biggest outdoor art festivals in the Southeast — hundreds of artists, live music, food vendors, and waterfront views. If you run a local business, this is a great place to network and soak up some creative energy.

When: February 28 – March 1 Where: Julian B. Lane Waterfront Park, Tampa Cost: Free admission

Green Thumb Festival — Coming in April (Mark Your Calendar)

Not technically March, but the Green Thumb Festival at Walter Fuller Park in St. Pete (April 25–26) is already generating buzz. If you're in landscaping, home services, or anything outdoors-related, start planning your presence now. Vendor spots fill up fast.

St. Pete Artwalk — Every 2nd Saturday

One of the best recurring events in Pinellas County. Over 40 studios and galleries across five arts districts open their doors from 5–9 PM on the second Saturday of each month. That means March 14 is your next chance.

Take the trolley, walk, or drive — it's a solid evening out and a great way to support local artists. If you're a business in the arts district, this is free foot traffic. Use it.

When: March 14, 5–9 PM
Where: Five arts districts across St. Petersburg
More info: stpeteartsalliance.org/artwalk

First Friday Art Walk — Gulfport

Every first Friday (so March 6), downtown Gulfport transforms into an open-air gallery from 5–9 PM. Live music, local vendors, and a laid-back beach-town vibe. It's smaller than St. Pete Artwalk but has its own charm — and the crowd is loyal.

When: March 6, 5–9 PM
Where: Beach Blvd, Downtown Gulfport

Pinellas Arts Village Walk — 4th Saturday

This one features the Pinellas Park Art Society, local studios, a sweet shop, and a pub. It's community-focused and walkable — exactly the kind of event that builds word-of-mouth for local businesses.

When: March 28, 4–8 PM
Where: Park Blvd, Pinellas Park

Creative Clay Outdoor Art Market — First Fridays

Great if you prefer daytime events. Local ceramics, paintings, and handmade goods.

When: March 6, 10 AM – 2 PM
Where: 1846 First Ave S, St. Petersburg

Indie Faire — Gulfport (3rd Saturday)

Handmade crafts, jewelry, pottery, locally sourced botanicals. It's the indie, artisan side of Pinellas and it draws a dedicated crowd.

When: March 21, 5–9 PM
Where: Beach Blvd, Gulfport

Whiskey Obsession Festival — Tampa

If you're into spirits (and let's be honest, who isn't), the Whiskey Obsession Festival hits Tampa on March 19. Details are still rolling out, but last year's event featured dozens of distilleries, live music, and food pairings. Keep an eye on this one.

When: March 19
Where: Tampa (venue TBA)

Breakaway Music Festival — April 17–18

Another one to mark early: Breakaway Music Festival at Raymond James Stadium. Major headliners, massive crowds, and a prime opportunity for Tampa Bay businesses to get in front of a younger demographic. Sponsorship and vendor applications are likely open now.

When: April 17–18
Where: Raymond James Stadium, Tampa

Why This Matters for Your Business

Here's the thing most business owners miss: local events are marketing opportunities. You don't need a booth at every festival. But you should:

  • Post about local events on your social media (Google loves local relevance)
  • Partner with event organizers for cross-promotion
  • Run event-day specials if you're near the venue
  • Update your Google Business Profile with event-related posts

Local SEO isn't just about keywords. It's about being part of the community. Google notices when your business is connected to what's happening in your area.
